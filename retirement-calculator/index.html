What Is the Standard Retirement Age?

The official retirement age varies by country and is often linked to when you can start receiving full state pension or social security benefits. Common retirement ages around the world include:

  • United States: Full retirement age is 67 for those born after 1960
  • United Kingdom: State pension age is currently 66, rising to 67 by 2028
  • Germany: Standard retirement age is 67
  • France: Legal minimum retirement age is 62
  • Australia: Age pension eligibility starts at 67
  • Canada: Standard retirement age is 65

Can I Retire Early?

Many people aim for early retirement through careful savings and investment strategies — sometimes called FIRE (Financial Independence, Retire Early). Use our calculator with a custom retirement age of 50, 55, or 60 to see how far away your early retirement date is.

Frequently Asked Questions

When can I retire in the US?
In the United States, the full retirement age for Social Security benefits is 67 for anyone born in 1960 or later. You can start receiving reduced benefits at 62, or delay until 70 for higher monthly payments.
How many years until I can retire?
Enter your date of birth and target retirement age above. The calculator will show you exactly how many years, months, and days remain until your retirement date.
What is FIRE (Financial Independence, Retire Early)?
FIRE is a financial movement where people aggressively save and invest with the goal of retiring much earlier than the traditional retirement age — often in their 40s or 50s. Use our calculator with a custom retirement age to plan your FIRE date.
