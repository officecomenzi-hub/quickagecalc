What Is Pensionable Age?

Pensionable age (also called retirement age or state pension age) is the minimum age at which you become eligible to receive state pension or Social Security benefits. It varies by country and is periodically adjusted upward as life expectancy increases.

How to Calculate Your Pensionable Age

Your retirement year is calculated by adding your country's pensionable age to your birth year. For example, if you were born in 1970 and live in the US, your pensionable age is 67 — meaning you can retire in 2037 (1970 + 67). If your birthday has already passed in that year, you retire that year. If not, you retire the following year.

Early Retirement vs Full Pensionable Age

Most countries allow early retirement with reduced benefits. In the United States, you can claim Social Security from age 62, but your monthly benefit is permanently reduced by up to 30% compared to waiting until full retirement age (67). Delaying beyond 67 increases your benefit by 8% per year up to age 70.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the pensionable age in the US?
In the United States, the full pensionable age (Social Security retirement age) is 67 for anyone born in 1960 or later. Early retirement is available from age 62 with permanently reduced benefits. Delaying past 67 increases your monthly benefit by 8% per year up to age 70.
How do I calculate my year of retirement?
Add your country's pensionable age to your birth year. For example: born 1968 in the UK (pensionable age 66) = retirement year 2034. If your birthday falls later in that year, you may not reach 66 until the following year — the calculator above accounts for this automatically.
When can I retire?
Your retirement date depends on your birth date and your country's pensionable age. Enter both above to get your exact retirement year, month, and how many years remain.
What is the pensionable age in the UK?
The UK State Pension age is currently 66 for both men and women. It is scheduled to rise to 67 between 2026 and 2028, and further increases are planned for the 2040s.
Can I retire before pensionable age?
Yes — most countries allow early retirement with reduced state pension benefits. In the US you can retire at 62 (reduced benefits), in Canada at 60 (CPP with reduction), and in the UK you can access private pensions from age 55. The calculator above shows your official state pensionable age.
How many years until I reach pensionable age?
Enter your birth date and country in the calculator above to get the exact number of years and months remaining until you reach your country's pensionable age.
Is pensionable age the same as retirement age?
Yes — pensionable age and retirement age are used interchangeably. Both refer to the age at which you become eligible for state pension or Social Security benefits. The official term varies by country.
